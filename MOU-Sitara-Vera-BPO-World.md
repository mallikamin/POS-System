## 2. SCOPE OF PARTNERSHIP

### 2.1 Phase 1: POS SaaS Platform

The initial scope of this partnership shall be the commercialisation of the Platform as a multi-vertical Point-of-Sale SaaS solution targeting the following sectors:

(a) Restaurants, food and beverage, and hospitality establishments;

(b) Oil and fuel station operations;

(c) Retail and general commerce.

The Platform shall include, without limitation, the following capabilities: multi-channel order management (dine-in, takeaway, call centre, delivery, and third-party aggregator integration), kitchen display systems, menu and inventory management, payment processing, QuickBooks accounting integration (Online and Desktop), FBR and PRA tax compliance and fiscal invoicing, multi-tenant deployment, role-based access control, and real-time reporting and analytics.

### 2.2 Future Phases: Additional Products

The Parties contemplate the development and commercialisation of additional software products beyond the POS Platform, including but not limited to:

(a) Human Resource Management Portal;

(b) Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems;

(c) Custom enterprise solutions tailored to specific client requirements.

Each such additional product shall be governed by a separate SOW or addendum to this MOU, which shall specify the scope, deliverables, timeline, intellectual property ownership, and commercial terms applicable to that product. For the avoidance of doubt, additional products developed at the request of BPO World (such as an HR Portal for a specific client) shall be scoped and charged on an upfront project basis to Sitara Vera's account per the agreed SOW, and shall not fall under the revenue-share model described in Section 4 unless expressly agreed otherwise in writing.

### 2.3 Exclusivity

This MOU does not grant BPO World exclusive rights to market or sell the Platform in any territory or vertical. Sitara Vera retains the unrestricted right to market, sell, license, and deploy the Platform to any client, in any territory, through any channel, whether directly or through other partners.

## 4. REVENUE MODEL

### 4.1 Revenue Share on BPO World-Sourced Clients

For Clients introduced, sourced, or acquired through the efforts of BPO World, recurring SaaS subscription revenue shall be shared between the Parties on a fifty percent (50%) to fifty percent (50%) basis, calculated on Net Revenue as defined in Section 4.2.

### 4.2 Net Revenue Defined

**"Net Revenue"** shall mean the gross recurring SaaS subscription payments received from a Client, less the following deductions:

(a) Cloud hosting and infrastructure costs directly attributable to that Client's usage (compute, storage, bandwidth, CDN);

(b) Payment gateway processing fees and transaction charges;

(c) Third-party API and software license costs directly attributable to that Client (including but not limited to QuickBooks API subscription fees, FBR integration fees, SMS gateway charges, and similar third-party service costs);

(d) Applicable taxes, duties, and government levies (including GST, sales tax, withholding tax, and any other statutory deductions).

Sitara Vera shall maintain transparent records of all such deductions and shall provide BPO World with a monthly statement detailing the calculation of Net Revenue for each Client.

### 4.3 Sitara Vera-Sourced Clients

For Clients sourced, acquired, or introduced independently by Sitara Vera through its own efforts, one hundred percent (100%) of all revenue shall belong to Sitara Vera. BPO World shall have no claim, right, or entitlement to any revenue from such Clients.

### 4.4 BPO World Independent Revenue

For services provided by BPO World independently of the Platform (i.e., services that do not utilise, integrate with, or relate to the Platform), one hundred percent (100%) of all revenue shall belong to BPO World. Sitara Vera shall have no claim to such revenue.

### 4.5 Custom Development Projects

Custom development projects requested by BPO World or its Clients that fall outside the standard Platform offering (including but not limited to bespoke HR portals, ERP modules, or one-off integrations) shall be scoped, quoted, and governed by a separate SOW. Such projects shall be charged on an upfront fixed-fee or time-and-materials basis, payable by BPO World (or the requesting Client, as agreed in the SOW) directly to Sitara Vera. The revenue-share model described in Section 4.1 shall not apply to custom development projects unless expressly agreed in the relevant SOW.

### 4.6 Payment Terms

(a) Revenue share settlements shall be calculated and paid on a monthly basis.

(b) Payment shall be made within fifteen (15) business days following receipt of the corresponding Client payment by the collecting Party.

(c) If either Party collects payment from a Client, it shall remit the other Party's share within the timeframe specified in sub-clause (b) above.

(d) All payments shall be made via bank transfer to the designated account of the receiving Party. Each Party shall provide the other with its banking details in writing.

(e) In the event of a disputed deduction, the disputing Party shall notify the other Party in writing within ten (10) business days of receiving the monthly statement. The Parties shall use good-faith efforts to resolve any such dispute within thirty (30) days.

### 4.7 Lifetime Revenue Share

The revenue share described in Section 4.1 shall apply for the lifetime of each BPO World-sourced Client relationship, for so long as this MOU remains in force. Upon termination of this MOU, Section 10 (Exit and Transition) shall govern the continued applicability of revenue share during the transition period.

### 4.8 Audit Rights

Each Party shall have the right, upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice and no more than once per calendar year, to engage an independent certified public accountant to audit the other Party's records relating to revenue, Client payments, and Net Revenue calculations under this MOU. The cost of such audit shall be borne by the requesting Party, unless the audit reveals a discrepancy of five percent (5%) or more in favour of the requesting Party, in which case the audited Party shall bear the cost of the audit and shall promptly remedy the discrepancy.

## 5. HOSTING AND INFRASTRUCTURE

### 5.1 Sitara Vera Control

The Platform shall be hosted, deployed, and operated on cloud infrastructure under the exclusive administrative control of Sitara Vera. Sitara Vera shall maintain sole access to all servers, databases, deployment systems, monitoring tools, and administrative consoles.

### 5.2 BPO World Resource Contributions

Any contribution of infrastructure resources by BPO World (including but not limited to AWS account access, credits, or subscription resources) shall be treated as a financial contribution equivalent to the market value of such resources. Such contributions shall be credited against BPO World's share of hosting cost deductions under Section 4.2(a). For the avoidance of doubt, any such contribution shall NOT entitle BPO World to administrative access, control, or visibility into the Platform's infrastructure, deployment pipelines, or server environments.

### 5.3 Data Sovereignty

Client data shall be stored on Sitara Vera-managed infrastructure. Where applicable regulatory requirements mandate that data be stored within the territory of Pakistan, Sitara Vera shall ensure compliance with such requirements by utilising data centres located within Pakistani jurisdiction or the nearest available compliant region (currently AWS Middle East - Bahrain). Specific data residency requirements for individual Clients shall be addressed on a case-by-case basis.

### 5.4 Data Ownership

For the avoidance of doubt, all Client data (including but not limited to transactional records, customer information, financial data, and operational data) is owned by the respective Client. Neither Sitara Vera nor BPO World shall claim ownership of Client data. Both Parties shall handle Client data in accordance with applicable data protection laws and the data processing terms agreed with each Client.

## 7. INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 PCI-DSS Compliance

(a) The Platform shall be developed and operated in accordance with the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS) guidelines applicable to its service provider category.

(b) The Platform shall NOT store, process, or retain full primary account numbers (PANs), card verification values (CVV/CVC), PINs, or magnetic stripe data.

(c) All payment card processing shall be delegated to PCI-DSS certified third-party payment gateway partners. The Platform shall interact with payment data only through tokenised references provided by such certified partners.

### 7.2 FBR/SRB/PRA Tax Compliance

(a) The Platform shall support the invoice formats and reporting requirements of the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR), the Sindh Revenue Board (SRB), and the Punjab Revenue Authority (PRA), as applicable to Clients in the respective jurisdictions.

(b) The Platform shall include a configurable tax calculation engine supporting jurisdiction-specific GST and sales tax rates, including the ability to configure multiple tax rates for different item categories.

(c) The Platform shall be integration-ready for FBR real-time invoice reporting when such reporting is mandated by regulatory authority.

### 7.3 Food Safety and Traceability (F&B Vertical)

(a) The Platform shall maintain timestamped audit trails for all orders from placement through fulfilment, supporting traceability requirements.

(b) The Kitchen Display System shall implement first-in-first-out (FIFO) order management principles.

### 7.4 Fuel Station POS (Oil Vertical)

(a) The Platform shall support shift-based operations, nozzle and pump mapping, and dip-stick inventory reconciliation features tailored to fuel station operations.

(b) The Platform shall be integration-ready for fuel dispensing hardware APIs, subject to hardware vendor cooperation and API availability.

### 7.5 Data Protection

(a) Client data shall be logically isolated through the Platform's multi-tenant architecture using tenant-level data segregation (tenant_id isolation). No Client shall have access to any other Client's data.

(b) Data shall be stored within Pakistani jurisdiction or the nearest compliant data centre region unless a Client explicitly requests otherwise in writing.

(c) Sitara Vera shall maintain regular encrypted backups with documented and tested recovery procedures.

### 7.6 QuickBooks Integration Compliance

(a) The Platform's QuickBooks integration shall be built using Intuit's official QuickBooks Online and Desktop APIs.

(b) Each Client shall connect their own QuickBooks account via OAuth 2.0 authorisation. Sitara Vera shall NOT store Client QuickBooks credentials. Only OAuth tokens (which are revocable by the Client at any time) shall be retained.

(c) The integration shall comply with Intuit's Terms of Service and API usage policies.

### 7.7 State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) Compliance

(a) Any digital payment features incorporated into the Platform shall comply with the State Bank of Pakistan's regulatory framework for electronic money institutions and payment system operators, as applicable.

(b) The Platform shall integrate only with SBP-licensed and regulated payment processors and financial institutions.

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 8.1 Confidentiality Obligations

Each Party agrees that during the term of this MOU and for a period of three (3) years following its termination or expiry, it shall:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information of the other Party in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party;

(c) Use the other Party's Confidential Information solely for the purposes of performing its obligations and exercising its rights under this MOU;

(d) Take all reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality of the other Party's Confidential Information, using at least the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar nature and importance, but in no event less than reasonable care.

### 8.2 Scope of Confidential Information

Without limiting the generality of Section 1.1, Confidential Information includes:

(a) Business strategies, plans, and projections;

(b) Client lists, Client identities, and Client-specific terms;

(c) Pricing structures, fee schedules, and revenue data;

(d) Technical architecture, source code, algorithms, and system designs;

(e) Financial statements, cost structures, and margin data;

(f) Employee and contractor information;

(g) The terms and conditions of this MOU itself.

### 8.3 Permitted Disclosures

The obligations set forth in Section 8.1 shall not apply to Confidential Information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault or breach by the receiving Party;

(b) Was already known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

(c) Is independently developed by the receiving Party without reference to or use of the disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

(d) Is received from a third party who is not bound by any obligation of confidentiality with respect thereto;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, court order, or order of a competent regulatory authority, provided that the receiving Party gives the disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permissible) and cooperates with the disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to obtain a protective order or similar relief.

### 8.4 Return or Destruction

Upon termination or expiry of this MOU, each Party shall promptly return or destroy (at the disclosing Party's election) all Confidential Information of the other Party in its possession, custody, or control, and shall certify in writing that it has done so. Notwithstanding the foregoing, each Party may retain copies of Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law or regulation, or embedded in routine electronic backup systems, subject to the continuing confidentiality obligations of this Section 8.

## 9. NON-COMPETE AND NON-CIRCUMVENTION

### 9.1 Non-Compete (BPO World)

During the term of this MOU and for a period of two (2) years following its termination or expiry, BPO World shall not, directly or indirectly, whether alone or in conjunction with or on behalf of any other person, firm, company, or entity:

(a) Develop, design, commission, fund, invest in, or operate a software platform that competes with the Platform or any material component thereof (including but not limited to POS systems, restaurant management systems, or multi-vertical SaaS platforms targeting the same industry verticals);

(c) Acquire, license, or white-label a competing platform from any third party for the purpose of marketing it to Clients in the target verticals covered by this MOU.

### 9.2 Non-Solicitation of Personnel

BPO World shall not, during the term of this MOU and for a period of two (2) years following its termination or expiry, directly or indirectly solicit, recruit, hire, or engage (whether as employees, contractors, consultants, or otherwise) any person who is or was an employee, contractor, or consultant of Sitara Vera at any time during the twelve (12) months preceding such solicitation, without the prior written consent of Sitara Vera.

### 9.3 Non-Circumvention

(a) Neither Party shall, directly or indirectly, circumvent, avoid, or bypass the other Party to deal directly or indirectly with any Client, prospect, contact, or business relationship introduced by the other Party, for the purpose of avoiding the revenue-sharing obligations or other commercial terms of this MOU.

(b) All Client introductions and referrals shall be documented in writing (email shall suffice) and acknowledged by the receiving Party.

### 9.4 Non-Solicitation of Clients (Mutual)

(a) Sitara Vera shall not, for a period of two (2) years following termination of this MOU, directly solicit Clients that were introduced by BPO World during the term of this MOU, for the purpose of migrating such Clients to direct Sitara Vera contracts at terms that exclude BPO World's revenue share; **provided, however**, that this restriction shall not apply if the termination of this MOU was caused by BPO World's material breach pursuant to Section 11.3.

(b) BPO World shall not, during the term of this MOU or at any time thereafter, direct, encourage, or assist any Client to migrate away from the Platform to a competing solution, or use its Client relationships to divert business away from the Platform.

## 10. TERM AND RENEWAL

### 10.1 Initial Term

This MOU shall become effective on the Effective Date and shall remain in force for an initial term of two (2) years (the **"Initial Term"**).

### 10.2 Renewal

Upon expiry of the Initial Term, this MOU shall automatically renew for successive periods of one (1) year each (each a **"Renewal Term"**), unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the expiry of the then-current term. The Initial Term and any Renewal Terms are collectively referred to as the **"Term"**.

### 10.3 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this MOU for any reason upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other Party.

### 10.4 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this MOU immediately upon written notice to the other Party if:

(a) The other Party commits a material breach of any provision of this MOU and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice specifying the breach and requiring its remedy (the **"Cure Period"**);

(b) The other Party becomes insolvent, is placed into receivership, files for or has filed against it a petition in bankruptcy, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or is wound up or dissolved;

(c) The other Party engages in fraud, gross misconduct, or illegal activity in connection with the subject matter of this MOU;

(d) A change of control occurs in the other Party (meaning any transaction or series of transactions resulting in a change of more than fifty percent (50%) of the beneficial ownership or voting control of such Party) without the prior written consent of the non-changing Party.

## 11. EXIT AND TRANSITION

### 11.1 Transition Period

Upon the effective date of termination or expiry of this MOU (whether by notice, non-renewal, or for cause), a transition period of twelve (12) months (the **"Transition Period"**) shall commence, during which the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to ensure an orderly wind-down of the partnership.

### 11.2 Obligations During Transition Period

During the Transition Period:

(a) **Existing Client Contracts**: All existing Client contracts and subscriptions that are active as of the date of termination notice shall continue to be serviced by both Parties in accordance with their respective obligations under Section 6;

(b) **Revenue Share Continuation**: The revenue-share arrangements under Section 4 shall continue to apply to all existing BPO World-sourced Clients for the duration of the Transition Period;

(c) **No New Acquisitions**: Neither Party shall acquire new Clients under the terms of this partnership during the Transition Period. Each Party shall be free to independently pursue new Clients on its own terms;

(d) **Client Handoff**: BPO World shall facilitate a warm handoff of Client relationships to Sitara Vera, including introductions, transition meetings, and reasonable cooperation to ensure continuity of service for Clients;

(e) **Data and Materials**: BPO World shall return all Platform-related marketing materials, demonstration environments, Client data, and Confidential Information to Sitara Vera in accordance with Section 8.4.

### 11.3 Post-Transition

Upon expiry of the Transition Period:

(a) All licenses granted to BPO World under Section 3 shall be fully and irrevocably revoked;

(b) BPO World shall immediately cease all marketing, sales, and support activities relating to the Platform;

(c) Sitara Vera shall continue servicing all existing Clients directly, without further obligation to BPO World;

(d) BPO World shall retain full rights to its independently-sourced non-Platform clients and services;

(e) All confidentiality obligations under Section 8 shall survive in accordance with their stated duration;

(f) All non-compete and non-solicitation obligations under Section 9 shall survive in accordance with their stated duration.

### 11.4 Shortened Transition for Cause

In the event of termination for cause under Section 10.4, the non-breaching Party may, at its sole discretion, shorten the Transition Period to a minimum of three (3) months by providing written notice to the breaching Party.

## 12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 12.1 Good-Faith Negotiation

In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this MOU, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof (a **"Dispute"**), the Parties shall first attempt to resolve the Dispute through good-faith negotiation between the authorised representatives of each Party (being Mr. Jawad Saleem for Sitara Vera and Mr. Younis Kamran for BPO World, or their respective designees). Such negotiation shall commence within seven (7) days of written notice of the Dispute and shall continue for a period of not less than thirty (30) days.

### 12.2 Mediation

If the Dispute is not resolved through negotiation within the period specified in Section 12.1, either Party may refer the Dispute to mediation under the rules and procedures of the Lahore Chamber of Commerce and Industry (LCCI). The mediation shall take place in Lahore, Pakistan, and shall be conducted in the English language. The Parties shall use their best efforts to conclude the mediation within thirty (30) days of referral.

### 12.3 Arbitration

If the Dispute is not resolved through mediation within the period specified in Section 12.2, either Party may refer the Dispute to final and binding arbitration in accordance with the Arbitration Act 1940 (as amended) of Pakistan. The arbitration shall be conducted as follows:

(a) **Seat**: Lahore, Pakistan;

(b) **Language**: English;

(c) **Arbitrator**: A sole arbitrator mutually agreed upon by the Parties. If the Parties fail to agree on an arbitrator within fifteen (15) days of the referral to arbitration, the arbitrator shall be appointed by the Lahore High Court upon the application of either Party;

(d) **Award**: The arbitral award shall be final and binding upon the Parties. Judgment upon the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### 12.4 Governing Law

## SCHEDULE A: PLATFORM DESCRIPTION

### A.1 Overview

The Platform is a multi-vertical, multi-tenant, cloud-hosted Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) Point-of-Sale and business management system. The Platform is accessed via web browser on any device (tablets, POS terminals, desktops, smartphones) and requires no client-side software installation.

### A.2 Core POS Capabilities

| Module | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Multi-Channel Order Management** | Dine-in (interactive floor plan with table management), Takeaway (token-based queue system), Call Centre (phone number lookup with customer history), Delivery (rider assignment and GPS tracking), Third-party aggregator integration (Foodpanda and similar platforms) |
| **Menu Engine** | Hierarchical categories and sub-categories, item management with images, modifier groups (size, toppings, customisations) with min/max selection rules, combo meals with configurable groups, multi-language support, scheduled availability |
| **Kitchen Display System (KDS)** | Per-station order routing based on category and item-level rules, colour-coded priority timers, bump-and-recall workflow, audio alerts, FIFO queue management |
| **Payment Processing** | Cash, credit/debit card, mobile wallet, and QR code payments; split-check capability; tip management; cash drawer management; end-of-day reconciliation |
| **Floor Plan Management** | Drag-and-drop table layout editor; multi-floor support; real-time table status (available, occupied, reserved, needs cleaning); shape-aware table rendering |
| **Reporting and Analytics** | Real-time dashboard with KPI cards; sales summary by period, channel, category, and item; hourly sales analysis; staff performance metrics; CSV and PDF export |
| **Staff and Access Control** | Role-based permissions (admin, manager, cashier, kitchen); PIN-based fast login for POS terminals; manager override for voids, discounts, and refunds; audit trail |
| **Tax Compliance** | Configurable tax groups and rates per jurisdiction; FBR and PRA invoice format support; fiscal invoicing readiness |
| **Accounting Integration** | QuickBooks Online and Desktop integration via official Intuit API; OAuth 2.0 Client-authorised access; configurable sync (per-order or daily summary); chart of accounts mapping |

### A.3 Technical Architecture

| Component | Technology |
|-----------|-----------|
| Frontend | React 18 + TypeScript + Tailwind CSS |
| Backend | FastAPI (Python) + SQLAlchemy 2.0 (async) |
| Database | PostgreSQL 16 (multi-tenant with tenant_id isolation) |
| Cache | Redis 7 (caching + pub/sub for real-time events) |
| Real-time | WebSockets with room-based broadcasting |
| Deployment | Docker containers on AWS ECS Fargate |
| Region | AWS Middle East (Bahrain) — closest to Pakistan |

### A.4 Multi-Vertical Adaptations

The Platform architecture supports vertical-specific configurations, including:

- **Restaurant/F&B**: Full KDS, table management, course-based ordering, modifier engine
- **Fuel Stations**: Shift-based operations, pump/nozzle mapping, dip-stick reconciliation, fuel dispensing hardware API readiness
- **Retail**: Barcode scanning, inventory management, purchase orders, customer loyalty

## SCHEDULE C: SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA) TARGETS

### C.1 Uptime Commitment

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Monthly Platform Availability | 99.5% |
| Planned Maintenance Window | Tuesdays 02:00-04:00 PKT (with 48-hour advance notice) |
| Excluded from Uptime Calculation | Planned maintenance windows, force majeure events, Client-side network issues, third-party service outages (e.g., payment gateway, QuickBooks API) |

### C.2 Uptime Calculation

Monthly Uptime Percentage = ((Total Minutes in Month - Downtime Minutes) / Total Minutes in Month) x 100

Where "Downtime" means any period during which the Platform is materially unavailable to Clients due to causes within Sitara Vera's reasonable control, excluding planned maintenance and the exclusions listed above.

### C.3 Incident Response Times

| Severity | Definition | Response Time | Resolution Target |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Critical (P1)** | Platform is completely unavailable or a critical business function is non-operational for all or most Clients | 1 hour | 4 hours |
| **High (P2)** | A major feature is impaired or a single Client's operations are significantly affected, with no workaround available | 4 hours | 12 hours |
| **Medium (P3)** | A feature is impaired but a workaround exists, or the issue affects a non-critical function | 8 business hours | 48 business hours |
| **Low (P4)** | Minor issues, cosmetic defects, feature requests, or general inquiries | 2 business days | Next scheduled release |

### C.4 Support Hours

| Support Type | Hours |
|-------------|-------|
| L1 Support (BPO World) | As agreed with each Client (typically business hours) |
| L2/L3 Technical Support (Sitara Vera) | Monday-Saturday, 09:00-21:00 PKT |
| Critical Incident (P1) Support | 24/7/365 |

### C.5 Data Backup and Recovery

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Backup Frequency | Daily full backup + continuous transaction log backup |
| Backup Retention | 30 days (daily snapshots) + 12 months (monthly archives) |
| Recovery Point Objective (RPO) | 1 hour (maximum data loss in worst case) |
| Recovery Time Objective (RTO) | 4 hours (time to restore service from backup) |
| Backup Testing | Quarterly recovery drill with documented results |
