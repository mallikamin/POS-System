# BPO World Partner Pricing Proposal

**Date:** March 7, 2026  
**Prepared by:** Sitara Vera  
**For:** BPO World Limited

## Positioning

This should be presented as a **commercial partnership proposal with pricing schedule**, not a one-time software sale quotation.

Reason:

- BPO World is acting as the **business/channel partner**
- Sitara Vera remains the **technology partner** and owner of the platform/IP
- Revenue is expected to come from **third-party client subscriptions and implementation**
- Custom work, integrations, and client-specific enhancements should be handled through separate SOWs

This structure is aligned with the draft MOU already in the project repository.

## Recommended Commercial Model

### 1. Revenue Share on BPO World-Sourced Clients

- **Recurring SaaS subscription revenue:** 50% Sitara Vera / 50% BPO World
- Split should be calculated on **Net Revenue**
- Net Revenue = client payments received minus:
  - hosting/infrastructure directly attributable to that client
  - payment gateway charges
  - third-party license/API costs
  - taxes and statutory deductions

This matches the current draft MOU and keeps the arrangement simple.

### 2. Upfront Implementation Fees

Implementation should **not** be casually bundled into the revenue-share pool. It funds real delivery work.

Recommended rule:

- If BPO World only introduces the client: **100% implementation fee to Sitara Vera**
- If BPO World handles discovery, onsite coordination, and first-line onboarding: **70% Sitara Vera / 30% BPO World**

### 3. Custom Development

Anything outside the standard POS platform should be quoted separately through an SOW.

- Client-specific customization
- new modules
- external integrations
- accounting or tax compliance work beyond baseline scope
- enterprise reporting changes

Recommended commercial rule:

- **100% payable to Sitara Vera**, unless a different split is agreed in the SOW

## Commercial Guardrails

These should be explicit in the proposal:

- Sitara Vera retains full ownership of source code, platform IP, architecture, and deployment systems
- BPO World receives a non-exclusive right to market and sell the platform
- BPO World handles L1/commercial relationship; Sitara Vera handles L2/L3 technical delivery
- Any end-client promised feature outside the current standard scope requires written approval from Sitara Vera
- Revenue share applies only to **BPO World-sourced clients**
- Clients sourced directly by Sitara Vera are excluded from the split

## Payment Terms

Recommended:

- 70% of implementation fee upfront at project start
- 30% of implementation fee before or at go-live
- recurring subscription billed monthly in advance
- revenue-share settlement between the parties within 15 business days of client payment receipt
- minimum client contract term: 12 months
- annual price revision: 10% to 12%

## Recommended Structure for BPO World Discussion

### Option A: Referral + Revenue Share

Best if BPO World mainly brings the client and manages relationship.

- recurring revenue: 50/50 on net revenue
- implementation: 100% Sitara Vera
- custom development: separate SOW

### Option B: Active Sales and Onboarding Partner

Best if BPO World also handles discovery, onsite coordination, and first-line onboarding.

- recurring revenue: 50/50 on net revenue
- implementation: 70% Sitara Vera / 30% BPO World
- custom development: separate SOW

### Option C: White-Label Reseller

Only use if strategically necessary. Highest risk.

- BPO World resells under its own commercial wrapper
- Sitara Vera still retains IP and infrastructure control
- requires tighter controls on pricing, collections, client registration, and support boundaries

This option should be avoided unless there is a strong reason.

## Recommended Negotiation Position

The cleanest position is:

1. Keep the **50/50 recurring SaaS split** for BPO World-sourced clients
2. Keep **implementation and custom work outside the standard revenue share**
3. Require a **signed client registration form** whenever BPO World introduces an account
4. Do not give away source code, hosting access, or unrestricted white-label rights
5. Price the platform as a **managed operating system**, not as a cheap generic POS
